## The Problem

The Phase 10 chore board shipped its full v1.0 spine â€” rooms, recurrence/decay, the claim state machine, first-class completion history â€” as a Svelte 5 island over Minimal API, and it is **deployed to production but dark** (`CHORES_USE_ISLAND` is unset, so `/chores` renders a "not enabled" placeholder). v1.0 deliberately shipped two pieces of **substrate with no consumer**: the `Chore.EffortPoints` column and the append-only `ChoreCompletion` log (`CompletedByUserId`, `CompletedAt`, `EffortPointsSnapshot` â€” `Data/Entities/ChoreCompletion.cs:1-22`). Those exist so v1.1 can build an **equity / household-load view** without new plumbing. Today nothing reads them, and the only equity-adjacent surface is a display-only held-chore-count strip in the island's Mine lens (`frontend/chores/src/lib/components/MineView.svelte`, the WP-12 "equity seed").

v1.1 turns that substrate into a feature and gives the board a reason to reach out. Two coupled deliverables:

1. **A first-class, visualized equity / household-load view** â€” per-member, **effort-weighted** distribution of completed work over a time window, computed server-side from the `ChoreCompletion` log, framed as a *neutral distribution of collective load* (never a leaderboard), with actionable context (what's falling behind, what's up for grabs).
2. **A weekly Discord digest** â€” the equity view's **delivery surface**. Each household optionally configures its own Discord webhook; on a weekly cadence a digest posts to that family's channel: the collective summary, what's falling behind, the unclaimed pile, and the neutral per-member distribution. The digest is the v1.0 philosophy â€” "the list holds the expectation so nobody has to nag" â€” *reaching out on a cadence* instead of waiting to be visited.

The real users are **Justin + Natalie (adults), Tristan (23), Samantha (18)**, plus **Trey's family (younger kids)** as a second household. The framing is locked from v1.0: **household engineering, non-punitive, escalate-to-visibility only.** This shapes the hardest constraint in this spec: a digest is a *push*, but it must remain a **collective, ambient broadcast to a shared channel â€” never an @-mention nudge or nag aimed at a person.**

This bundle ships **dark and whole**: every piece (equity view, digest engine, plus the go-live table stakes below) is built behind the existing flag and shipped as **one drop** with `CHORES_USE_ISLAND` flipped on at the end. The operator's explicit preference is a complete, coherent experience over an incremental half-feature (the family previewed the v0 UX prototype; under-delivering would underwhelm). Two **go-live table stakes** therefore ride inside the bundle:

- **Edit-chore dialog** â€” `updateChore`/`PUT /api/chores/{id}` is wired in `frontend/chores/src/lib/api.ts` but no island UI drives it; v1.0 can create/claim/complete/delete a chore but not *edit* one.
- **Backfill for existing households** â€” `SeedData.SeedChoresAndRoomsAsync` (`Data/SeedData.cs:135`) only fires on *new* household creation, so the operator's existing household has the schema but an empty board. Flipping the flag onto a blank, un-editable board would be the under-delivery to avoid.

The **architecture seam** v1.1 introduces is a scheduler. v1.0 has none (Must-Not MN1: no `BackgroundService`/`IHostedService`). A weekly digest fires on a cadence, so v1.1 adds an **authenticated trigger endpoint driven by external cron** â€” *not* an in-process hosted service. This is the single place v1.1 deliberately reverses a v1.0 must-not, and it is called out as an explicit ratification gate.

All v1.0 locked decisions and constraints live in the Phase 10 workshop (`D:/Development/data/outputs/workshops/phase10-chore-task-management/`); this spec extends that spine and must not relitigate it.

## What "Solved" Looks Like

A household member opens `/chores` and, alongside the four v1.0 lenses (Needs-attention / Rooms / Up-for-grabs / Mine), finds a fifth first-class lens â€” **Equity** (a.k.a. "Household load"). It renders a **server-computed, effort-weighted distribution**: over the current week, how the household's completed effort-points are spread across members, shown as proportions (bars/shares), with a neutral "equal share" reference, plus the count of what's currently *falling behind* and *up for grabs* so the view drives action rather than mere scorekeeping. It never ranks, shames, or labels anyone "behind." A member with no completions this week simply shows a low share â€” stated as fact, not judgment. The numbers come from one new endpoint (`GET /api/chores/equity?window=week`) computed in a pure, timezone-aware service mirroring `ChoreStatusCalculator`; the island renders handed values and **never does client-side date math** (M5/M6).

Once a week, on the configured day/time, a **digest posts to the household's Discord channel**: a friendly collective headline ("The house knocked out 23 chores / 41 effort-points this week ðŸ’ª"), the neutral per-member distribution, what's falling behind, and how many chores are up for grabs â€” all framed as shared visibility, with **no @-mentions and no targeted nudges**. A household configures this on a small **digest-settings surface**: paste a Discord webhook URL, toggle enabled, pick the weekly send day/time. The webhook is stored **per-household, encrypted at rest** (via the app's existing Data Protection), and **never logged**. A household that configures nothing simply gets no digest â€” the second family (Trey's) opts in if and when they want. The send is driven by an **external cron** job hitting `POST /api/chores/digest/run`, authenticated by a **shared-secret token** (not the user cookie), idempotent so a double-fire never double-posts within a cadence window, and iterating only households that are enabled, configured, and due.

A member can finally **edit a chore** (name, room, recurrence, effort, owner, photo) via a dialog reachable from the chore card, driving the already-wired `PUT`. Existing households can **load the starter set** (the idempotent seed) so the board isn't empty on launch. The whole thing builds through the existing Viteâ†’MSBuildâ†’Docker island pipeline, applies one additive EF migration via the existing auto-migrate path, holds multi-tenant isolation on every new endpoint, and â€” as the final go-live act â€” flips `CHORES_USE_ISLAND` on.

*(Surface placement validated against the v1.0 IA: the Equity lens is a peer in the same client-side view-switcher over a separate equity payload; it does not perturb the frozen board DTO / `board.json` contract â€” M9.)*

## Hidden Assumptions Surfaced

- **[ASSUMPTION: A1] Outbound resilience is `Microsoft.Extensions.Http.Resilience` (`AddStandardResilienceHandler` on a named `HttpClient`), not raw Polly.** The recipe scraper uses `builder.Services.AddHttpClient("RecipeScraper").AddStandardResilienceHandler(...)` (`Program.cs:142-157`); `RecipeScraperService` just resolves `CreateClient("RecipeScraper")`. The Discord sender mirrors this with a named `"DiscordWebhook"` client + its own resilience options (shorter timeouts; Discord rate-limits webhooks ~30/min).
- **[ASSUMPTION: A2] Encrypting the webhook URL is a *new* use of an already-registered capability.** `AddDataProtection().SetApplicationName("FamilyCoordinationApp").PersistKeysToFileSystem(...)` is configured (`Program.cs:24-59`) and `DATAPROTECTION_CERT` is **required in production** â€” but Data Protection is currently used only for auth cookies; no stored value is encrypted today. v1.1 injects `IDataProtectionProvider`, creates a named protector (e.g. `CreateProtector("ChoreDigestWebhook")`), and stores the ciphertext in the new entity. No new package, no new infra.
- **[ASSUMPTION: A3] The equity read model must be a *separate* endpoint with its own contract fixture.** The board DTO (`Services/Dtos/ChoreDtos.cs`) is frozen and pinned by `board.json` (M9). Adding equity aggregates to it would churn that contract + the island `types.ts` in lockstep. A new `GET /api/chores/equity` + a new `equity.json` fixture keeps the board contract untouched (E2/E3-safe).
- **[ASSUMPTION: A4] Equity windows need TZ-correct day boundaries.** "This week" is a local-calendar concept; reuse the injected `TimeZoneInfo` (`ResolveChoresTimeZone`, `Program.cs:407-436`) + `TimeProvider` exactly as `ChoreStatusCalculator` does. Computed server-side; the island renders handed values (the global `new Date('YYYY-MM-DD')` footgun stays on the .NET side).
- **[ASSUMPTION: A5] The dev seed must gain multiple members + cross-member completions to exercise equity locally.** `SeedChoresAndRoomsAsync` currently authors all completions as the single first household user (`Data/SeedData.cs`), so an equity view over it renders a one-person "distribution" â€” meaningless. The dev seed is extended to seed additional household members (realistic names) and backdated completions spread across them. (Production backfill stays single-user-safe; this multi-member enrichment is dev-only.)
- **[ASSUMPTION: A6] The digest fires via external cron, not a `BackgroundService`.** This reverses MN1. The trigger is an HTTP endpoint; the *schedule* lives in cron (a `.env`-configured system cron / pm2 cron on the darktower curls the endpoint). The endpoint forecloses nothing â€” a future `BackgroundService` could call the same builder/sender â€” so the reversal is minimal and reversible. **Explicit ratification gate (see decisions D-fire).**
- **[ASSUMPTION: A7] The trigger endpoint authenticates with a shared-secret token via header, not the cookie, and not a query param.** Cron has no user session, so the run endpoint sits outside the cookie-auth `RequireAuthorization()` groups and validates a `CHORES_DIGEST_TRIGGER_TOKEN` (env, read via `builder.Configuration`) presented in a request **header** (query params leak into access logs / proxies). Comparison is fixed-time (avoid timing oracles). No logged-in user can trigger digests.
- **[ASSUMPTION: A8] Backfill reuses the idempotent seed.** `SeedChoresAndRoomsAsync` already no-ops if the household has any rooms or chores, so exposing it as a "load starter set" action (admin/settings-triggered) for an existing household is safe and re-runnable.
- **[ASSUMPTION: A9] Going live is a deploy-time flag flip, not code.** `CHORES_USE_ISLAND=true` in the prod `.env.local` + redeploy/restart is the final act of the "one drop"; the bundle does not auto-enable.
- **[ASSUMPTION: A10] No roles are required.** Equity aggregates over existing `User` rows; the digest is household-wide. Neither needs the deferred `MemberRole` scaffold, so escalation E1 stays un-tripped. (If "only adults see the equity view" were wanted, that would change â€” explicitly out of scope.)

## Open Questions (guide refinement, do not block)

- **OQ1 â€” Cadence default + send window.** Weekly is locked; which local day/time is the default (e.g. Sunday 18:00)? Stored per-household, tunable; pick a default constant.
- **OQ2 â€” Equity windows.** Just `week`, or also `all-time` / a `last-week` comparison? The endpoint takes a `window` param; v1.1 should ship at least `week` (others are cheap additions if the view wants them).
- **OQ3 â€” "Fair share" reference.** A neutral equal-share line (`100%/N members`) is descriptive, not prescriptive â€” is that in, or is even a reference line too close to judgment? (Leaning: include the neutral reference; no thresholds/labels.)
- **OQ4 â€” "Due" determination in the run endpoint.** How does the endpoint decide a household is due â€” `lastSentAt + 7d` elapsed, or day-of-week match + not-yet-sent-today? (Leaning: day-of-week + send-hour match AND not already sent in this cadence window, so cron can safely fire hourly.)
- **OQ5 â€” Equity surface placement.** A 5th lens in the ViewSwitcher (cleaner, matches "first-class") vs. a panel inside Mine (less prominent). (Leaning: 5th lens.)
- **OQ6 â€” Digest content depth.** Minimum viable embed vs. a richer one (per-room dirtiness, streaks). Keep v1.1 to: collective headline + per-member distribution + falling-behind + up-for-grabs. (Streaks/rooms are v1.2-safe.)
